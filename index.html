FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is InveStar? InveStar empowers creators to build their fan communities and enable fans to own a share in their success, giving fans an opportunity to become a part of Creator’s exclusive community. The platform gives the fans a chance to own a share of creator’s income and get special privileges like exclusive content, behind the scenes content, meet & greet opportunities, backstage access, social hangouts, virtual interactions and much more.
How does it work? 1. Creators launch their Card collections on Investor that users can purchase to support and back their favourite creators.
2. Card holders get access to exclusive Creator communities, access to exclusive experiences that money can’t buy and a share of Creator’s growth
3. User collect cards to boost their rank and participate in various contests to win exciting rewards.
How does InveStar help creators? For the first time, Creators get the ability to establish meaningful relationships with their fans. Creators can now convert their fans into shareholders giving rise to new avenues of monetisation for creators and fans alike.
What do I get after purchasing a card? Each Card on InveStar represents:
A license for non-commercial use of the digital art associated with the fan card
A share of future royalty (provided the creator has included royalty share in the card)
Access to reward points programme. Reward points are determined by the tier of the Fan Card.
Any additional tier specific perks that the creator chooses to include.
What is royalty on InveStar? Royalty Share is income generated by the creator from the asset such as YouTube channel, Spotify streams, song etc included in the Card. Royalty shares are paid out to Card holders from time to time once creator gets paid. Royalty payouts happen every 3 to 6 months on an average depending on the creator.

Once available, Royalty Share is available to Card holders in their INR wallet. Users can transfer the Royalty Share to their bank accounts easily.
How do I pay on InveStar? You can pay with any of the standard payment methods i.e. UPI, Credit or Debit Card, Netbanking etc.
